\chapter{Bottom-Up Coordination under Local Temporal Logic Tasks}
\label{chap:bottom-up}

Centralized automata-based planning scales poorly when a global temporal
specification is synchronized with the joint transition system of all robots.
The resulting product construction grows with both team size and task
complexity, and becomes especially restrictive under incomplete information,
inter-robot dependence, and online model changes. This chapter therefore adopts
a bottom-up perspective: each robot is assigned a local temporal task, and
coordination is induced by relations among these local tasks rather than by a
single monolithic team specification.
The central question is how local temporal tasks can be planned, coordinated,
and revised while preserving the logical structure needed for correct team
behavior. Section~\ref{ch03:sec:task_relaxation_adaptation} studies task
relaxation and plan adaptation under incomplete workspace information.
Section~\ref{ch03:sec:dep-local-collab} then introduces dependent local tasks
with collaborative actions, where one robot's execution may require functional
support from others. Together, these developments extend discrete planning from
isolated local execution to structured bottom-up coordination.
