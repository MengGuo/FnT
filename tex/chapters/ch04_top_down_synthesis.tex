\chapter{Top-Down Synthesis for Global Temporal Logic Tasks}
\label{chap:top-down}

The previous Chapter~\ref{chap:bottom-up} developed a bottom-up coordination
framework in which temporal tasks are assigned locally and execution-time
couplings are resolved as they arise. In contrast, many missions are specified
from the outset at the level of the whole team. The central problem is then to
decompose, distribute, and refine a global temporal specification while
preserving its logical structure and keeping the resulting planning problem
tractable for heterogeneous teams and large workspaces.
This chapter develops a top-down synthesis framework for such global tasks.
Section~\ref{ch04:sec:po_decomp_assignment} converts accepted automaton
behaviors into subtask occurrences and compact temporal-dependence structures
for assignment. Section~\ref{ch04:subsec:task} extends the construction to
large fleets through hierarchical abstraction. Section~\ref{ch04:sec:online_continual_tasks}
then addresses continual operation, where new demands require online revision
without discarding the executed prefix or the global temporal structure.
