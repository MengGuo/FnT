\section{Summary}
\label{sec:ch06_summary}

Chapter~\ref{chap:uncertainty} has examined how dynamic constraints and
environmental uncertainty reshape multi-robot planning at several
levels of abstraction.
Section~\ref{ch06:sec:ch06_safe_plan} addressed probabilistic temporal
tasks under safety and safe-return requirements through hierarchical
abstractions and policy synthesis.
Section~\ref{ch06:sec:ch06_uncertain} then considered reactive
coordination under dynamic temporal tasks, emphasizing online adaptation
to moving targets and evolving task information.
Finally, Sec.~\ref{ch06:sec:ch06_stl} developed a continuous
trajectory-optimization perspective based on collaborative optimal
transport, in which STL robustness, geometric safety, and inter-robot
couplings are incorporated into a unified planning framework.
Taken together, these sections show that effective multi-robot planning
under uncertainty requires the coordinated use of symbolic abstraction,
reactive replanning, and continuous optimization, depending on whether
the dominant challenge lies in probabilistic task evolution, dynamic
task geometry, or tightly coupled motion synthesis.
