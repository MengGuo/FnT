\section{Dependent Local Tasks with Collaborative Actions}
\label{sec:dep-local-collab}

This section considers a heterogeneous multi-robot system indexed by
$i \in \mathcal{N} \triangleq \{1,\cdots,N\}$. Each robot is assigned a
local task, yet the execution of some task-relevant actions may require
real-time assistance from neighboring robots. The goal is to retain the
local-task viewpoint developed earlier, while introducing an explicit
model for action dependence and an online coordination mechanism that
resolves such dependence during execution.

The main distinction from the setting of independent local tasks is that
feasibility is no longer determined solely by a single robot model.
Instead, a local plan may contain collaborative actions whose completion
depends on assisting actions provided by nearby robots. This section
formalizes that dependence at the action level, derives an offline local
plan for each robot, and then introduces an online protocol that
requests, assigns, and repairs collaborations in a distributed manner.
The presentation follows the order of definition, computation,
algorithmic realization, and analysis.

\subsection{Robot models and dependent actions}
\label{subsec:dep-model}

The workspace is partitioned into a finite set of regions
$\Pi \triangleq \{\pi_1,\cdots,\pi_M\}$. For each robot $i$, mobility is
described by a weighted transition system
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{M}_i \triangleq
\left(
\Pi,\,
\rightarrow_{\mathcal{M}}^i,\,
\pi_{\mathcal{M},0}^i,\,
\Psi_{\mathcal{M}}^i,\,
L_{\mathcal{M}}^i,\,
T_{\mathcal{M}}^i
\right),
\label{eq:dep-mobility}
\end{equation}
where $\rightarrow_{\mathcal{M}}^i \subseteq \Pi \times \Pi$ is the
motion relation, $\pi_{\mathcal{M},0}^i \in \Pi$ is the initial region,
$L_{\mathcal{M}}^i$ labels each region by relevant propositions, and
$T_{\mathcal{M}}^i$ assigns a positive transition time. The set
$\mathcal{N}_i \subseteq \mathcal{N}$ denotes the neighbors of robot
$i$, namely the robots with which direct communication is available.

\begin{definition}[Action model]
\label{def:dep-action-model}
Robot $i$ is associated with three action sets. The set $\Sigma_l^i$
contains local actions that can be completed by robot $i$ alone. The set
$\Sigma_c^i$ contains collaborative actions that are initiated by robot
$i$ but require external assistance. The set $\Sigma_h^i$ contains
assisting actions that robot $i$ can provide to others. Define
$\Sigma_a^i \triangleq \Sigma_l^i \cup \Sigma_c^i$ and
$\Sigma_i \triangleq \Sigma_a^i \cup \Sigma_h^i$. The idle action
$\sigma_0 \triangleq \texttt{None}$ belongs to $\Sigma_l^i$.

The action model of robot $i$ is the tuple
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{B}_i \triangleq
\left(
\Sigma_i,\,
\Psi_{\Sigma}^i,\,
L_{\Sigma}^i,\,
\texttt{Cond}_i,\,
\texttt{Dura}_i,\,
\texttt{Depd}_i
\right),
\label{eq:dep-action-model}
\end{equation}
where $L_{\Sigma}^i$ labels active actions, $\texttt{Cond}_i$ encodes
region conditions for action execution, $\texttt{Dura}_i$ gives the
action duration, and $\texttt{Depd}_i$ specifies external assistance.
More precisely, $\texttt{Depd}_i(\sigma)=\emptyset$ for all
$\sigma \in \Sigma_l^i \cup \Sigma_h^i$, while
$\texttt{Depd}_i(\sigma_c) \subseteq \Sigma_h^{\sim i}$ for every
$\sigma_c \in \Sigma_c^i$, where
$\Sigma_h^{\sim i} \subseteq \bigcup_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \Sigma_h^j$
collects the assisting actions that may be supplied by the neighbors of
robot $i$.
\end{definition}

Definition~\ref{def:dep-action-model} places dependence between actions,
not between robot identities. This choice is important. A collaborative
action does not require a prescribed robot, but only the availability of
the required assisting actions. The resulting model is therefore more
robust to team reconfiguration and robot failures.

\begin{definition}[Completion of actions]
\label{def:dep-action-done}
Let $\pi \in \Pi$. A local or assisting action
$\sigma \in \Sigma_l^i \cup \Sigma_h^i$ is said to be completed at
region $\pi$ if
$\texttt{Cond}_i(\sigma,L_{\mathcal{M}}^i(\pi))=\top$ and the action is
maintained for $\texttt{Dura}_i(\sigma)$ time units. A collaborative
action $\sigma_c \in \Sigma_c^i$ is completed at region $\pi$ if the
same two conditions hold and, in addition, every assisting action in
$\texttt{Depd}_i(\sigma_c)$ is completed at the same region by other
robots.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Complete robot model]
\label{def:dep-complete-model}
Given $\mathcal{M}_i$ and $\mathcal{B}_i$, the complete model of robot
$i$ is the weighted transition system
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{R}_i \triangleq
\left(
\Pi_{\mathcal{R}}^i,\,
\rightarrow_{\mathcal{R}}^i,\,
\pi_{\mathcal{R},0}^i,\,
\Psi_{\mathcal{R}}^i,\,
L_{\mathcal{R}}^i,\,
T_{\mathcal{R}}^i
\right),
\label{eq:dep-complete-model}
\end{equation}
where $\Pi_{\mathcal{R}}^i \triangleq \Pi \times \Sigma_i$,
$\pi_{\mathcal{R},0}^i \triangleq \langle \pi_{\mathcal{M},0}^i,
\sigma_0 \rangle$, and
$\Psi_{\mathcal{R}}^i \triangleq
\Psi_{\mathcal{M}}^i \cup \Psi_{\Sigma}^i$. A transition in
$\rightarrow_{\mathcal{R}}^i$ has one of the following forms.

First, motion is allowed from
$\langle \pi,\sigma_0 \rangle$ to
$\langle \pi',\sigma_0 \rangle$ whenever
$\pi \rightarrow_{\mathcal{M}}^i \pi'$. Second, an admissible action
$\sigma \neq \sigma_0$ may be activated at a fixed region $\pi$ from
$\langle \pi,\sigma_0 \rangle$ to $\langle \pi,\sigma \rangle$ whenever
$\texttt{Cond}_i(\sigma,L_{\mathcal{M}}^i(\pi))=\top$. Third, after an
action is executed, the model returns from
$\langle \pi,\sigma \rangle$ to $\langle \pi,\sigma_0 \rangle$. The
weight function $T_{\mathcal{R}}^i$ assigns the corresponding motion
time or action duration.
\end{definition}

The task of robot $i$ is an sc-LTL formula
$\varphi_i$ over $\Psi_{\mathcal{R}}^i$. As in Chapter~2, such a task
admits satisfaction by a finite prefix and can therefore be handled by a
finite accepting path construction \citep{baier2008principles,
gastin2001fast}. The problem addressed in this section is stated next.

\begin{problem}
\label{prob:dep-local-tasks}
For each robot $i \in \mathcal{N}$, given the complete model
$\mathcal{R}_i$ and the local task $\varphi_i$, design a distributed
coordination and control scheme such that every task $\varphi_i$ is
satisfied, even when some task-relevant actions require online
collaboration.
\end{problem}

\subsection{Offline local synthesis}
\label{subsec:dep-offline}

The first computation is purely local. For each robot $i$, construct the
automaton $\mathcal{A}_{\varphi_i}$ associated with $\varphi_i$ and form
the product automaton
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{A}_{p,i} \triangleq
\mathcal{R}_i \otimes \mathcal{A}_{\varphi_i},
\label{eq:dep-product}
\end{equation}
where the product is defined exactly as in the finite-prefix synthesis
machinery introduced earlier. Accepting paths of
$\mathcal{A}_{p,i}$ correspond bijectively to finite executions of
$\mathcal{R}_i$ whose traces satisfy $\varphi_i$
\citep{baier2008principles,guo2013motion}.

Let $\mathfrak{R}_{p,i}^{\mathrm{acc}}$ denote the set of finite
accepting runs of $\mathcal{A}_{p,i}$. The local planning problem is
\begin{equation}
R_{p,i}^{\star} \in
\arg\min_{R \in \mathfrak{R}_{p,i}^{\mathrm{acc}}}
\texttt{Cost}_i(R),
\label{eq:dep-offline-opt}
\end{equation}
where $\texttt{Cost}_i(R)$ is the total accumulated weight along the
run. The projection of $R_{p,i}^{\star}$ onto
$\Pi_{\mathcal{R}}^i$ yields the initial motion-action plan
$\tau_{i,\mathrm{init}}$. This local synthesis is standard shortest-path
search on the product graph and can be implemented with Dijkstra-type
methods \citep{lavalle2006planning,guo2013motion}.

The plan $\tau_{i,\mathrm{init}}$ is task-correct with respect to
$\varphi_i$, but it is not yet execution-ready when collaborative
actions appear in the plan. The remaining computations must therefore be
performed online.

\subsection{Finite-horizon coordination}
\label{subsec:dep-online}

At execution time $t$, let the current state of robot $i$ belong to the
plan $\tau_i$. The notation $\tau_i$ refers to the current plan, which
is initialized as $\tau_{i,\mathrm{init}}$ and later updated by online
repair. Let $\ell_i(t)$ be the current index of robot $i$ in $\tau_i$.
Given a planning horizon $H_i>0$, define
\begin{equation}
k_i(t) \triangleq
\min
\left\{
k \ge \ell_i(t)\, \middle|\,
\sum_{s=\ell_i(t)}^{k-1}
T_{\mathcal{R}}^i\bigl(\tau_i[s],\tau_i[s+1]\bigr) \ge H_i
\right\},
\label{eq:dep-horizon-index}
\end{equation}
where $k_i(t)$ is set to the last plan index if the set in
\eqref{eq:dep-horizon-index} is empty.

The segment $\tau_i[\ell_i(t):k_i(t)]$ is the predicted execution within
the horizon. If this segment contains no collaborative action, robot $i$
simply executes the next motion or local action. Otherwise, let
$\langle \pi_i^{\star}(t),\sigma_i^{\star}(t)\rangle$ be the first state
in the horizon such that $\sigma_i^{\star}(t) \in \Sigma_c^i$. Its
predicted execution time, measured from the current instant, is denoted
by $T_i^{\star}(t)$.

The corresponding request message is
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{Req}_i(t) \triangleq
\left\{
\bigl(\sigma,\pi_i^{\star}(t),T_i^{\star}(t)\bigr)
\,\middle|\,
\sigma \in \texttt{Depd}_i\bigl(\sigma_i^{\star}(t)\bigr)
\right\},
\label{eq:dep-request}
\end{equation}
which is broadcast to all robots in $\mathcal{N}_i$. Only the first
collaborative action in the horizon is considered, since the outcome of
the current coordination step may alter all subsequent timing.

\subsubsection{Reply computation}
\label{subsubsec:dep-reply}

Suppose robot $j \in \mathcal{N}_i$ receives a request for assisting
action $\sigma$ at region $\pi_i^{\star}(t)$. If robot $j$ is already
committed to another collaboration, it returns an infeasible reply.
Otherwise, it evaluates whether its current accepting suffix can be
modified so as to visit the state
$\langle \pi_i^{\star}(t),\sigma \rangle$ and still terminate at an
accepting state.

Let $\widehat{R}_{p,j}^{-}$ denote the current product suffix of robot
$j$ from its present state to its accepting state, and let
$\bar{c}_j \triangleq \texttt{Cost}_j(\widehat{R}_{p,j}^{-})$. For each
candidate state $q$ in the product automaton whose projection equals
$\langle \pi_i^{\star}(t),\sigma \rangle$, define
\begin{equation}
J_{j,\sigma}(q) \triangleq
\left|c_{j,\rightarrow}(q)-T_i^{\star}(t)\right|
+
\alpha_j
\Bigl(
c_{j,\rightarrow}(q)+c_{j,\leftarrow}(q)-\bar{c}_j
\Bigr),
\label{eq:dep-balanced-cost}
\end{equation}
where $c_{j,\rightarrow}(q)$ is the shortest-path cost from the current
product state of robot $j$ to $q$, computed while avoiding intermediate
collaborative and assisting actions, and $c_{j,\leftarrow}(q)$ is the
shortest-path cost from $q$ to the accepting product state in the
reversed graph. The scalar $\alpha_j>0$ balances temporal matching
against additional local cost.

The reply of robot $j$ is generated by minimizing
\eqref{eq:dep-balanced-cost}. If the minimum is attained, robot $j$
stores the corresponding revised suffix and returns feasibility together
with the candidate assisting time
$t_{j,\sigma} \triangleq c_{j,\rightarrow}(q^{\star})$. Otherwise, it
returns infeasibility. This computation is a pair of shortest-path
searches on the product graph and its reverse
\citep{lavalle2006planning,baier2008principles}.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:dep-helper}
If robot $j$ returns a feasible reply for assisting action $\sigma$,
then the revised suffix constructed by the reply computation reaches
$\langle \pi_i^{\star}(t),\sigma \rangle$ at time $t_{j,\sigma}$ and
still terminates at an accepting state of $\mathcal{A}_{p,j}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The forward shortest path ends at a state projecting to
$\langle \pi_i^{\star}(t),\sigma \rangle$. The backward shortest path in
the reversed product graph certifies reachability from that state to the
accepting state in the original graph. Their concatenation is therefore
a valid accepting suffix. The stated arrival time equals the weight of
its forward part by construction.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{Assignment and confirmation}
\label{subsubsec:dep-assign}

Once all replies are collected, robot $i$ assigns one helper to each
requested assisting action. Introduce a binary variable $x_{j,\sigma}$
that is equal to $1$ if robot $j$ is chosen to provide assisting action
$\sigma$, and equal to $0$ otherwise. The completion time of the
collaborative action is denoted by $f_i(t)$ and is obtained from
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\min_{x_{j,\sigma},\, f_i(t)} \quad & f_i(t) \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& f_i(t) \ge T_i^{\star}(t), \\
& f_i(t) \ge t_{j,\sigma} - M\bigl(1-x_{j,\sigma}\bigr),
\quad \forall j \in \mathcal{N}_i,\ \forall \sigma, \\
& \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} x_{j,\sigma} = 1,
\quad \forall
\sigma \in \texttt{Depd}_i\bigl(\sigma_i^{\star}(t)\bigr), \\
& \sum_{\sigma \in \texttt{Depd}_i(\sigma_i^{\star}(t))}
x_{j,\sigma} \le 1,
\quad \forall j \in \mathcal{N}_i, \\
& x_{j,\sigma} \in \{0,1\},
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:dep-assignment}
\end{equation}
where $M$ is a sufficiently large constant
\citep{wolsey1998integer}. Equation~\eqref{eq:dep-assignment} enforces
that every required assisting action is assigned exactly once, while
each helper serves at most one role in the same collaboration.

If \eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is feasible, robot $i$ sends confirmation
messages to the selected helpers and preserves its own current suffix.
Each confirmed helper replaces its current suffix by the revised suffix
computed in the reply stage. If \eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is infeasible,
the collaborative action is delayed and the local suffix of robot $i$ is
recomputed.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Online coordination and repair for robot $i$}
\label{alg:dep-online}
\DontPrintSemicolon
\KwIn{$\tau_i$, current state index $\ell_i(t)$, $H_i$, $D_i$}
\KwOut{Updated plan $\tau_i$}

Compute $k_i(t)$ from \eqref{eq:dep-horizon-index}\;
Locate the first collaborative action in $\tau_i[\ell_i(t):k_i(t)]$\;

\If{no collaborative action appears}{
  execute the next motion or local action and \textbf{return}\;
}

Construct and broadcast $\mathbf{Req}_i(t)$ from
\eqref{eq:dep-request}\;

\ForEach{reply from robot $j \in \mathcal{N}_i$}{
  evaluate candidate insertions by minimizing
  \eqref{eq:dep-balanced-cost}\;
}

Solve the assignment problem \eqref{eq:dep-assignment}\;

\If{\eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is feasible}{
  confirm the selected helpers and update their plan suffixes\;
}
\Else{
  delay the collaborative action by $D_i$ and recompute the local suffix
  of robot $i$\;
}

Execute the updated plan prefix\;
Monitor acknowledgments from confirmed helpers until the collaborative
action is completed\;

\If{a confirmed helper fails}{
  reassign the missing assisting action by
  \eqref{eq:dep-reassign}\;
}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Plan revision and failure recovery}
\label{subsec:dep-repair}

If the assignment problem \eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is infeasible,
robot $i$ postpones the collaborative action by a delay parameter
$D_i>0$ and recomputes only the remaining suffix of its local plan. The
revised suffix still starts from the current product state and still
terminates at an accepting state. The modification therefore preserves
local task correctness while moving the collaborative action to a later
time. This repair is repeated only when necessary.

Failure handling is equally local. Suppose that robot $i$ has already
confirmed a helper for an assisting action $\sigma$, but the helper no
longer acknowledges status inquiries. Robot $i$ treats that helper as
failed, requests the missing assisting action again, and selects the new
helper by
\begin{equation}
\hat{\jmath} \triangleq
\arg\min_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i^{\mathrm{avail}}}
\left| t_{j,\sigma} - f_i(t) \right|,
\label{eq:dep-reassign}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{N}_i^{\mathrm{avail}}$ is the set of neighbors that
return feasible replies for $\sigma$. The selected robot is then
confirmed and executes the corresponding revised suffix. This repair
keeps the original collaborative completion time whenever that remains
possible, and otherwise perturbs the schedule minimally.

\subsection{Analysis}
\label{subsec:dep-analysis}

The proposed protocol is intended for loosely coupled teams, in which
collaborations are local and occur only intermittently relative to the
full execution horizon.

\begin{assumption}
\label{ass:dep-loose}
There exists a finite constant $\overline{T}>0$ such that, whenever a
robot requests a collaborative action at time $t$, either the assignment
problem \eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is immediately feasible or it becomes
feasible after finitely many delay-and-repair iterations and within
$t+\overline{T}$.
\end{assumption}

Assumption~\ref{ass:dep-loose} does not require permanent availability
of assistance. It only requires eventual availability within a bounded
time window. This is the natural condition under which bottom-up
coordination remains compatible with finite local-task satisfaction.

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:dep-correctness}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:dep-loose}, the coordination scheme described
in Algorithm~\ref{alg:dep-online} solves
Problem~\ref{prob:dep-local-tasks}. In particular, each robot completes
its local task $\varphi_i$ in finite time.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The offline plan $\tau_{i,\mathrm{init}}$ is the projection of an
accepting run of $\mathcal{A}_{p,i}$, hence it satisfies $\varphi_i$.
Every motion transition and every local action in that plan requires
only local execution and therefore completes in finite time.

Consider a collaborative action encountered during execution. If
\eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is feasible, the selected helpers can realize
their assisting actions at the confirmed times by
Lemma~\ref{lem:dep-helper}, and the collaborative action therefore
completes at time $f_i(t)$. If \eqref{eq:dep-assignment} is infeasible,
the action is delayed and the local suffix is repaired without leaving
the accepting set of the product automaton. By
Assumption~\ref{ass:dep-loose}, this delay-repair process yields a
feasible assignment after finitely many repetitions and within finite
time.

The same argument applies after a helper failure, because the failed
assisting action is simply converted into a new local request and is
reassigned by \eqref{eq:dep-reassign}. Since every local plan contains
only finitely many actions, and each collaborative action becomes
feasible after finitely many repairs, the total execution time is
finite. Hence every task $\varphi_i$ is satisfied in finite time.
\end{proof}

The online computation is also modest. The reply stage for one helper
requires two shortest-path computations on the local product automaton,
with complexity of Dijkstra type
$\mathcal{O}(|\delta_{p,i}|\log|Q_{p,i}|)$
\citep{lavalle2006planning}. The assignment stage involves
$|\mathcal{N}_i| \cdot
|\texttt{Depd}_i(\sigma_i^{\star}(t))|$ binary variables and is solved
locally by the requesting robot \citep{wolsey1998integer}. This
confirms that the proposed mechanism scales with local neighborhood size
and local action dependence, rather than with the size of the entire
team. Compared with centralized or cluster-level formulations
\citep{chen2012formal,guo2012infeasible}, the computation remains
bottom-up and execution-driven.
