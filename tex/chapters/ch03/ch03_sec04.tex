\label{sec:ch03_case_study}

The bottom-up coordination framework is illustrated next on a
representative multi-robot example involving local temporal tasks,
execution-time dependence, and collaborative actions. The case study is
intended to show how local plan synthesis, request-reply exchange, and
online adaptation interact in a concrete setting.

\section{Summary}
\label{sec:ch03_summary}

This chapter developed a bottom-up view of multi-robot coordination
under local temporal tasks. Section~\ref{ch03:sec:task_relaxation_adaptation}
addressed task relaxation, safety-aware plan revision, online sensing,
and collaborative knowledge transfer in partially known workspaces.
Section~\ref{ch03:sec:dep-local-collab} then introduced dependent local
tasks with collaborative actions and a distributed execution-time
coordination mechanism for request, reply, assignment, and adaptation.
Taken together, these developments show how correctness can be
maintained without a monolithic global mission, by coordinating locally
specified tasks whose interactions arise during execution.
