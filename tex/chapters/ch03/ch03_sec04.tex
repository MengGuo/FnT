\section{Summary}
\label{sec:ch03_summary}

This chapter extended the planning framework from region-based motion tasks to
robot behaviors that involve both task-level actions and multi-robot
coordination. A modular construction was first introduced to integrate mobility
and action models into a complete robot abstraction suitable for temporal-logic
based synthesis. Building on this model, a distributed coordination scheme was
then developed for multi-robot systems with dependent local tasks, including
initial plan synthesis, on-line request and reply exchange, collaborator
selection, plan adaptation, and failure recovery. Under the assumption of
loosely coupled interactions, the proposed scheme ensures that all local tasks
are completed in finite time. The chapter therefore establishes a unified
framework for motion, action, and collaboration under formal task
specifications.
