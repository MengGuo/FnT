\chapter{Partial Knowledge and Limited Communication}
\label{chap:communication}


The preceding chapters developed coordination frameworks for local and global
temporal tasks under timely information exchange. In many deployments this
assumption is fragile: workspace knowledge is partial, communication is
range-limited or intermittent, and task-relevant information is obtained only
during execution. Temporal-task planning must therefore account not only for
motion, action, and allocation, but also for how information is acquired,
propagated, and used by the team.

This chapter studies multi-robot coordination under partial knowledge and
limited communication. Section~\ref{ch05:sec:ch05_interm_team} plans task
execution and communication events jointly. Section~\ref{ch05:sec:intermittent_comm}
develops an intermittent-communication exploration framework for delayed or
sparse information sharing. Section~\ref{ch05:sec:ch05_human} incorporates
online human requests through topology adaptation while preserving the formal
task structure. Section~\ref{sec:ch05_case_study} presents case studies, and
Section~\ref{sec:ch05_summary} summarizes the chapter.
